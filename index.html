What is MoodWave?

MoodWave is an adaptive environment system that uses a real Arduino sensor board to measure your room's temperature, humidity, noise level, and brightness, then turns those readings into a living music response matched to how the space actually feels. No guessing. No timers. The room tells MoodWave what it needs and MoodWave plays it.

♪

The Science Behind MoodWave

Research in environmental psychology shows that temperature, light, sound pressure, and humidity all directly influence attention span, stress hormones, creative output, and physical energy. MoodWave maps those four signals onto a mood model, classifies the room condition, and selects a music profile engineered to support — or counteract — what the environment is doing to the people inside it.

🌡️ Temperature → Energy & Fatigue
💧 Humidity → Heaviness & Comfort
🔊 Noise → Overload or Drive
💡 Brightness → Focus or Calm
♪

How It Works

  1. An Arduino sensor board reads temperature, humidity, noise, and brightness in real time.
  2. Python ingests and cleans the raw sensor stream.
  3. JavaScript updates the live dashboard every few seconds.
  4. MoodWave classifies the room — hot and stuffy, overstimulated, dim and quiet, bright and alert, or balanced.
  5. The music engine picks the best ambient profile for your chosen goal.

    Why Does It Matter?

    Your environment makes decisions before you do. A hot, stuffy room drains focus. A loud, chaotic room spikes cortisol. A dim, quiet room can numb motivation or invite deep rest. MoodWave measures all of that and responds with sound — the one environmental lever that never requires you to leave your chair.
